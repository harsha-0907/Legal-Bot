## Summary of the Supreme Court Judgment in IL and FS Engineering and Constructions Company Ltd. v. M/s. Bhargavarama Constructions & Ors.

**1. Constitutional References:** The judgment doesn't explicitly refer to any specific articles of the Indian Constitution. However, the case involves the interpretation of the Code of Civil Procedure (CPC), which is enacted under the authority of the Indian Constitution.

**2. Key Legal Questions:**
- Whether the High Court erred in allowing the impleadment of A.P. Transco as a party to the appeal and the original suit based on an application filed by the original defendants.
- Whether the High Court acted properly in setting aside the trial court's judgment and decree solely based on the impleadment of a new party.

**3. Legal Doctrines:**
- **Impleadment of Parties:** The court examines the principles governing the impleadment of parties in an appeal and a suit.
- **Dominus Litis:** The court recognizes the plaintiff's role as the dominus litis (master of the suit).
- **Procedure in First Appeal:** The court discusses the proper procedure for deciding a first appeal under Section 96 and Order XLI Rule 31 of the CPC.

**4. Outcome:** The appeal was allowed. The Supreme Court set aside the High Court's order impleading A.P. Transco and its order quashing the trial court's judgment and decree. The matter was remanded back to the High Court for further proceedings.

**5. Reliefs or Orders:**
- The High Court's order impleading A.P. Transco as a party to the appeal and original suit was set aside.
- The High Court's order quashing the trial court's judgment and decree was also set aside.
- The matter was remanded back to the High Court to decide the appeal on its merits.
- Respondent Nos. 1 and 2 were ordered to pay exemplary costs of Rs. 25,000/- to the State Legal Services Authority of the concerned High Court within four weeks.

**6. Reasoning:**
- The Supreme Court found that the High Court's decision to set aside the trial court's judgment solely based on the impleadment of A.P. Transco was improper and violated the principles of procedural fairness.
- The court emphasized that impleadment of parties in an appeal is not a straightforward process and requires careful consideration of its maintainability and the specific grounds for allowing it.
- The court also highlighted the plaintiff's right as dominus litis and emphasized that non-joinder of parties was not raised before the trial court.
- The court referred to its own earlier decision in K. Karuppuraj v. M. Ganesan, emphasizing that the High Court cannot dispose of a first appeal without proper discussion, assigning reasons, and raising points for determination as per Order XLI Rule 31 of the CPC.

**7. Statutory Interpretation:**
- The court interpreted Section 96 and Order XLI Rule 31 of the CPC regarding the proper procedure for deciding first appeals. 

**8. Precedents Cited:**
- K. Karuppuraj Vs. M. Ganesan, Civil Appeal Nos.6014-6015 of 2021 decided on 04.10.2021

**9. Legal Arguments Accepted/Rejected:**
- The court accepted the appellant's argument that the High Court acted improperly by setting aside the trial court's judgment solely based on impleadment.
- The court rejected the respondent's argument that the High Court acted correctly in allowing impleadment and setting aside the judgment.
