## Summary of the Judgment in United India Insurance Co. Ltd. v. Sushil Kumar Godara

**1. Constitutional References:**

* Article 141 of the Constitution of India (regarding the binding nature of Supreme Court judgments).

**2. Key Legal Questions:**

* Whether the insurer is liable to pay a claim for a stolen vehicle when the vehicle was being driven without a valid registration at the time of the theft.
* Whether the principle established in Narinder Singh (supra) regarding the non-liability of the insurer in case of an accident involving a vehicle without valid registration applies to a claim for theft.

**3. Legal Doctrines:**

* Contract law, specifically breach of contract and fundamental breach.
* Statutory interpretation of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, particularly Section 39 and Section 192.
* Binding precedent.

**4. Outcome:**

* The Supreme Court allowed the appeal and set aside the orders of both the National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (NCDRC) and the Rajasthan State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission.
* The respondent's complaint was dismissed.

**5. Reliefs or Orders:**

* The insurer was relieved of its liability to pay the insurance claim.

**6. Reasoning:**

* The Court found that the respondent's vehicle was being driven without a valid registration at the time of the theft, which constituted a fundamental breach of the insurance policy conditions.
* The Court held that the principle established in Narinder Singh (supra) applies to both accidents and theft claims, as driving without valid registration is a fundamental breach of the policy in both scenarios.
* The Court emphasized the binding nature of its previous judgment in Narinder Singh (supra) and criticized the NCDRC for ignoring this precedent.

**7. Statutory Interpretation:**

* The Court interpreted Section 39 and Section 192 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, emphasizing that driving a vehicle without a valid registration is a punishable offense.
* The Court held that this violation constitutes a fundamental breach of the insurance contract.

**8. Precedents Cited:**

* Narinder Singh Vs. New India Assurance Co. Ltd. (2014) 9 SCC 324 
* Naveen Kumar Vs. National Insurance Company Ltd. [RP/250/2019] decided on 26.11.2019

**9. Legal Arguments Accepted/Rejected:**

* The appellant argued that the respondent's lack of a valid registration constituted a fundamental breach of the policy, allowing the insurer to repudiate the claim. This argument was accepted by the Court.
* The respondent argued that Narinder Singh (supra) was not applicable to theft claims and that the temporary registration, though expired, was sufficient. This argument was rejected.
* The respondent argued that the NCDRC had correctly dismissed the appeal based on the facts of the case. This argument was rejected.
