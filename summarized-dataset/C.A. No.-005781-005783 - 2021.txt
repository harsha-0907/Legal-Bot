## Summary of the Judgment in Surya Educational and Charitable Trust v. M/s Setia Builders Engineers and Contractors

**1. Constitutional References:**

* None explicitly stated.

**2. Key Legal Questions:**

* Whether the costs imposed by the High Court on the respondent for the delay in filing an appeal were excessive.

**3. Legal Doctrines:**

* **Condonation of Delay:** The Court considered the principles of condonation of delay in filing an appeal, specifically considering the reasons for the delay and the potential for prejudice. 
* **Principles of Justice and Fairness:** The Court impliedly considered the principles of justice and fairness in its decision to reduce the costs imposed by the High Court. 

**4. Outcome:**

* The Supreme Court partly allowed the appeals and modified the High Court's order by reducing the costs imposed on the respondent.

**5. Reliefs or Orders:**

* The costs imposed on the respondent were reduced to Rs. 3 Lakh.
* The respondent was given three weeks to pay the reduced costs. 
* The respondent's application for condonation of delay would be dismissed if the reduced costs were not paid within the stipulated time.

**6. Reasoning:**

* The Court, with the consent of the parties, considered the respondent's request to reduce the costs imposed by the High Court for the delay in filing an appeal. 
* The Court considered the facts and circumstances of the case and found that the costs imposed by the High Court were excessive.
* The Court exercised its discretion to modify the costs to ensure fairness and prevent undue hardship for the respondent.

**7. Statutory Interpretation:**

* The court did not explicitly interpret any specific statute. The decision implicitly applies principles of procedural fairness and the power of the Supreme Court to modify judgments passed by lower courts. 

**8. Precedents Cited:**

* None explicitly cited.

**9. Legal Arguments Accepted/Rejected:**

* The respondent argued that the costs imposed by the High Court were excessive. This argument was implicitly accepted by the Court.
* The appellant did not oppose the modification of costs. 

**Conclusion:**

The Supreme Court's judgment demonstrates the Court's willingness to ensure that judgments are just and equitable, even when it involves modifying orders passed by lower courts.  The decision highlights the Court's discretionary power to reduce costs in appropriate circumstances, preventing undue burden on litigants while upholding the importance of filing appeals within a reasonable timeframe.
