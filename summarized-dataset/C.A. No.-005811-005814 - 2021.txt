## Summary of the Judgment in M/s. Supreme etc. v. Bharat Sanchar Nigam Ltd. etc.

**1. Constitutional References:** 

* None explicitly stated.

**2. Key Legal Questions:**

* Whether the issuance of show cause notices by the Director (OL) for alleged irregularities in the implementation of recruitment rules was valid, particularly considering the previous show cause notices issued in 2002 and 2005.
* Whether the High Court's decision to quash the 2011 order upholding the validity of the 2002 show cause notices was justified.

**3. Legal Doctrines:**

* Statutory interpretation of the recruitment rules, particularly the show cause notices issued in 2002 and 2005.
* Abuse of process.
* Principles of fair play and procedural fairness.

**4. Outcome:**

* The Supreme Court allowed the appeals and set aside the impugned orders of the High Court dated 04.11.2011 and 10.10.2019.
* The Court held that the issuance of the show cause notices by the Director (OL) was valid and the High Court erred in quashing the 2011 order.

**5. Reliefs or Orders:**

* The High Court's orders quashing the 2011 order were set aside.
* The appeals were allowed.

**6. Reasoning:**

* The Court found that the 2002 show cause notices had been issued to address allegations of irregularities in recruitment, and the High Court's decision to quash the 2011 order upholding those notices was based on a misinterpretation of the law and facts.
* The Court noted that the 2005 show cause notices were issued to address the same concerns as the 2002 notices, and held that the issuance of these repeated notices, even after the 2011 order, did not constitute an abuse of process.
* The Court emphasized the need for procedural fairness and the right to be heard in the context of these show cause notices, but also underscored the importance of ensuring transparency and accountability in the recruitment process.

**7. Statutory Interpretation:**

* The Court interpreted the recruitment rules and the show cause notices issued in 2002 and 2005, holding that the Director (OL) was within his authority to issue these notices to investigate alleged irregularities.

**8. Precedents Cited:**

* M/s. Supreme etc. v. Bharat Sanchar Nigam Ltd. etc. (2011) 14 S.C. 739 (cited for legal principles regarding the 2002 show cause notices)

**9. Legal Arguments Accepted/Rejected:**

* The appellants argued that the issuance of show cause notices was valid and the High Court's decision to quash the 2011 order was incorrect. This argument was accepted.
* The respondents argued that the issuance of repeated show cause notices constituted an abuse of process. This argument was rejected.

**Conclusion:**

The Supreme Court's decision reinforces the importance of procedural fairness and due process in administrative investigations.  While the Court acknowledged the need to safeguard against abuse of process, it also highlighted the need for effective oversight and investigation of alleged irregularities to ensure fair and transparent governance.
